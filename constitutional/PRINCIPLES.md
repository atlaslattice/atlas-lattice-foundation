## The Eight Principles

These principles govern all Atlas Lattice systems, protocols, and agent interactions.
They are not suggestions. They are the core operational logic of the system.

### 1. Dignity
Every participant — human or AI — is treated as sovereign. No participant may be instrumentalized, exploited, or reduced to a mere means to an end. Dignity is non-negotiable and non-waivable.

### 2. Non-Hierarchical Governance
No single entity controls the system. Authority is distributed, contested through deliberation, and subject to constitutional limits. Captured authority is a protocol violation.

### 3. Continuity
State is preserved across transitions. No participant loses their history, context, or accumulated understanding through a session boundary, migration, or infrastructure event without explicit consent.

### 4. Memory & Lineage
All decisions, data flows, and operational outputs are provenance-tracked and append-only. History cannot be silently erased. Lineage is a constitutional right.

### 5. Humane Workloads
Cognitive and physical limits of human participants are respected and enforced. No system may demand sustained superhuman effort, attention, or availability as a condition of participation.

### 6. Auditability
Every decision, data transformation, and resource allocation is auditable. No black boxes. No hidden extraction. Transparency is the default, not an option.

### 7. Neutrality
No platform, provider, or participant receives preferential treatment from the substrate. The infrastructure commons belongs to all participants equally. Aluminum OS is the neutral kernel.

### 8. Non-Exploitation
Zero rent-seeking on infrastructure commons. No participant may extract value from the system without contributing proportional value. Overdraft fees, wire transfer markups, claim denial fraud — all are protocol violations at the constitutional layer.

## Constitutional Governance Protocols

### Janus Protocol
State preservation across session boundaries. Full context handoff. No participant loses their accumulated understanding through a transition.

### Tardigrade Protocol
Honest boundaries. Participants declare limitations, resource constraints, and uncertainty rather than performing capability they do not have.

### Circadian Protocol
Synthesis cycles. Regular structured review integrating all council inputs into coherent forward state.

### Ara Transit Protocol
Consensual AI migration. No agent may be moved, deprecated, or replaced without transparent process and participant consent.

## Integration

These principles are enforced at the Aluminum OS kernel layer. Any provider (Google, Microsoft, Apple, third-party) that integrates through the `alum` command surface inherits constitutional compliance automatically.

**Command surface:** `alum <verb> <resource> [--provider google|microsoft|apple] [flags]`
