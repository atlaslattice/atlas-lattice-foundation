## I. THE BASELINE: Why India Needs GRII

### Fertilizer Import Dependency (Current)

|---|---|---|---|

### The Hormuz Vulnerability

As of March 2026, India is conducting a sweeping supply chain audit because:
- **90% of India's LPG imports** transit the Strait of Hormuz
- **Fertilizer imports** also depend on West Asian natural gas (feedstock for urea/ammonia)
- **Freight rates** have risen ten-fold to account for passage risk
- **Kharif sowing season** is approaching — fertilizer availability is literally a food security issue

### REAL-WORLD VALIDATION (March 27, 2026)

**Crisil Report (this week):** Middle East conflict could cut domestic urea and complex fertilizer production by **10-15%**. Key findings:
- ~20% of urea and one-third of complex fertilizers (primarily DAP) are imported.
- 60-65% of LNG (urea feedstock) and 75-80% of ammonia come from the Middle East.
- Fertilizer subsidy bill could rise by **Rs 20,000-25,000 crore**.
- Inventory (~3 months) cushions the immediate blow, but prolonged crisis threatens kharif season.

**Union Budget 2026-27:** Rs 1.71 lakh crore allocated to the Department of Fertilizers — the largest subsidy line item. Redirecting even 10% (Rs 17,000 crore) toward FOM-linked incentives would fully fund the IBA's proposed Rs 10,000 crore capital subsidy for Bio-CNG nodes.

**Emergency diversification underway:** India has locked in 2.2 million tonnes of US LPG for 2026, Argentine supply doubled, and the first Iranian LPG cargo in years arrived via the *Aurora* (rupee payment). The Emergency Bridge is not theoretical — it is operational.

### CRITICAL UPDATE: HORMUZ CLOSURE (March 27, 2026)

**The Strait of Hormuz is physically closed.** Following US-Israeli strikes on Iran (February 28) and IRGC closure declaration (March 2), the scenario this document warned about is now reality.

**Confirmed impact on India:**
- **52% of India's oil imports** transit Hormuz (S&P Global) — not just LPG, but crude oil
- **Brent crude surged 50%** — from $80 to $120/barrel in under a week
- **India had 9-25 days of strategic reserves** at time of closure
- **Indian refiners pivoted to Russian oil** — deals for 60%+ of 100M barrels in Russian floating storage
- **US Treasury 30-day waiver** (March 6) permitted India to purchase stranded Russian cargoes
- **Iran declared India a "friendly nation"** (March 26) — safe passage granted through Hormuz for Indian-flagged vessels

**Fertilizer supply chain now at acute risk:**
- India imports **25% of its urea** and relies on West Asia for **86% of the natural gas** needed for domestic urea production
- Both supply lines transit Hormuz — the double vulnerability is now active
- The Rs 1.71 lakh crore fertilizer subsidy budget may be insufficient if crisis persists past kharif sowing season

**CBG blending mandate — the domestic alternative is LIVE:**
- Mandatory 1% CBG blending in CNG/PNG commenced FY 2025-26
- Scaling to 3% (FY 2026-27), 4% (2027-28), 5% (2028-29)
- Government authorized **biogas injection into national gas pipeline** (March 21, 2026)
- IBA pushing for **20% CBG blending in Delhi** (March 23, 2026)
- 120+ large-scale CBG plants operational; 500+ under development
- Projected sector investment: **USD 600 million** in 2026-27

**The thesis of this document is no longer theoretical. India's fertilizer sovereignty crisis is live, and the domestic substitution pathway (CBG + FOM) now has both policy infrastructure and market urgency.**

## II. THE PROVEN MODEL: Banas Bio-CNG Plant (GRII Node in Operation)

India already has a fully operational GRII node — the Banas Dairy plant in Banaskantha, Gujarat. Running successfully for six years.

### Operational Metrics (Single Plant)

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| **Dung processing capacity** | 100 metric tonnes/day |
| **Bio-CNG output** | ~1,800 kg/day |
| **Solid organic fertilizer** | 25 metric tonnes/day |
| **Liquid organic fertilizer** | 75 metric tonnes/day |
| **Daily revenue** | ₹3 lakh (~$36,000) |
| **Annual revenue** | ₹12 crore (~$1.4M) |
| **CO₂e emissions reduction** | ~6,750 tonnes/year |
| **Farmers supplied** | 400-450 families (20-25 villages) |
| **Dung price paid** | ₹1/kg (steady supplementary income) |

### Economic Multipliers

1. **Farmer income:** ₹1/kg for dung creates predictable revenue for 400-450 families per plant.
2. **Local employment:** 13 tractor-trolleys per plant for collection/transport.
3. **Revenue diversification:** Gas + solid fertilizer + liquid fertilizer = three revenue streams from one waste input.
4. **Capital cost:** ₹50-55 crore per 100 TPD plant — payback period 4-5 years based on ₹12 crore annual revenue.

### Government Commitment

Gujarat allocated **₹60 crore** in March 2026 budget for Bio-CNG plants. 10 new plants planned through cooperative milk societies. Banas model being adopted by nearly 15 states through Union Ministries of Jal Shakti and Cooperation.
