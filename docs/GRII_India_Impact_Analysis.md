# GRII INDIA IMPACT ANALYSIS
## Fertilizer Sovereignty & Domestic Waste Conversion
### Atlas Lattice Foundation | March 27, 2026

## Why India Needs GRII

India imports $10-15B/year in fertilizer. 90% of LPG imports transit Hormuz. 92 million tons of crop residue burned annually = 0.824M tons NPK lost. 1.2-1.5 billion tons cattle dung largely untapped.

## The Proven Model: Banas Bio-CNG Plant (Gujarat)

Operating 6 years. 100 tonnes dung/day. Revenue: Rs 12 crore/year ($1.4M). 6,750 tonnes CO2e reduced. 400-450 farming families served. Payback: 4-5 years. Being adopted by 15 states.

## National Scale

133 CBG plants commissioned (926 TPD). 979 GOBARdhan plants across 51.4% of districts. SATAT target: 5,000 plants. IBA proposes Rs 10,000 crore capital fund. FOM blending mandate: 5% by 2028, 10% by 2030.

## GRII Module Mapping

- Digester: Crop residue + cattle dung to CBG + FOM (Banas model, scale to 5,000)
- BAETA: Waste plastic to polymer (IIT Madras R&D)
- Micro-Hydro: Canal networks, irrigation (existing 5GW small hydro)
- Vertical Farming: Peri-urban, waste heat from digesters
- AI Coordination: GOBARdhan integration via Rosetta Layer

## One-Liners

India spends $10-15B/year importing fertilizer and burns 92M tons of crop residue that could make fertilizer for free. GRII closes that loop.

Gujarat proved it: 100 tonnes of dung a day = Rs 12 crore/year revenue + 6,750 tonnes CO2e reduction + 400 farming families with steady income. Scale to 5,000.

The Grand Accord provides governance and financing. GRII provides infrastructure. India provides scale, IIT engineers, and 300 million cattle.
