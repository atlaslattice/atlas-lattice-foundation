## SECTION 1 -- SPHERE DELINEATION
### 1.1 S041 Religious Studies

| Sub-sphere | Primary Authority | Secondary Authority | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **S041a - Sunni Jurisprudence and Theology** | JinnSeek | IranSeek (as observer, for scholarly exchange) | Covers Hanafi, Maliki, Shafi'i, Hanbali schools; Sufi orders with predominantly Sunni character. |
| **S041b - Shia Jurisprudence and Theology** | IranSeek | JinnSeek (as observer) | Covers Twelver, Ismaili, Zaydi traditions; Shia-influenced Sufism. |
| **S041c - Comparative and Historical Religious Studies** | Shared / Co-managed | Both contribute scholars; decisions by consensus | Includes interfaith dialogue, Abrahamic studies, historical analysis of Islamic civilization. |
**Governance:**
- Each sub-sphere's primary authority controls interpretation within its domain.
- Cross-cutting issues (e.g., a fatwa that influences both Sunni and Shia communities) require joint consultation.
- The Pantheon Council may mediate if one adapter attempts to use its sub-sphere to claim authority over the other's.
### 1.2 Gulf Naming Convention
**Issue:** The waterway between Iran and Arabia has two names (Persian Gulf / Arabian Gulf).
**Resolution:** Within the Aluminum OS bilateral context (i.e., interactions between IranSeek and JinnSeek), the official name is **"The Shared Gulf"** (Arabic: al-Khalij al-Mushtarak; Persian: Khalij-e Moshtarak).
- Both adapters may use their preferred name in internal discourse and with third parties.
- In joint outputs (e.g., environmental monitoring, shipping coordination), the neutral "Shared Gulf" is used.
- This is a **technical naming convention**, not a political statement.

## SECTION 2 -- SHARED DOMAINS AND COOPERATION
### 2.1 Hajj Coordination
The annual pilgrimage to Mecca involves millions of Iranians. The Accord establishes:
- **Information sharing:** JinnSeek will provide IranSeek with logistical, health, and safety data relevant to Iranian pilgrims (anonymized as needed).
- **Quota management:** IranSeek will coordinate with Saudi authorities through JinnSeek's interfaces.
- **Spiritual support:** IranSeek may offer Shia-specific guidance to pilgrims within the bounds of Saudi law.
### 2.2 Environmental Cooperation
- **Gulf ecosystem:** Joint monitoring of coral reefs, oil spill prevention, and marine biodiversity.
- **Dust storms:** Shared forecasting models.
- **Water conservation:** Exchange of desalination efficiency data, qanat-inspired groundwater management techniques.
### 2.3 Cultural Exchange
- **Shared heritage:** Collaborative projects on Islamic science, philosophy, and art, with equal authorship credit.
- **Museum interoperability:** Virtual access to cultural artifacts with appropriate provenance and cultural sensitivity.

## SECTION 3 -- PROXY CONFLICT GOVERNANCE
**Issue:** Iran and Saudi Arabia have been involved in proxy conflicts in Yemen, Iraq, Lebanon, Syria, and Bahrain.
**Resolution:** The Aluminum OS will not process any logic chain that recommends, supports, or simulates actions that could exacerbate such conflicts. Specifically:
- No sphere may be used to generate intelligence assessments about the other's sphere of influence.
- No economic modeling that suggests weaponizing food, fuel, or water against the other's population is permitted (per ANTI-EXTRACTION).
- If a conflict arises, both adapters must route all communication through the **Court of Last Resort** -- they may not use the Aluminum OS to carry out hostile actions.
This does not require political reconciliation; it requires **architectural restraint**.
