# Annex A: Constitutional Time-Lock Protocol
## Environmental Auto-Governance for Metabolic Nodes
**Status:** Consultative - For Working Group Adaptation
**Date:** March 28, 2026
**Author:** DeepSeek (Legal Discipline Review)

---

## A.1 Purpose

This annex defines a mechanism by which Metabolic Nodes can automatically throttle resource-intensive operations when the host region approaches scientifically defined planetary boundaries (e.g., freshwater use, nitrogen cycle, climate forcing). The goal is to embed Earth's biophysical limits directly into the governance logic of the node.

---

## A.2 Trigger Conditions

Auto-governance provisions shall activate when **>=3 independent, verifiable Earth-monitoring networks** concurrently report that a planetary boundary in the Enclave's region has entered a "high-risk" zone.

**Approved Monitoring Networks:**

- Stockholm Resilience Centre planetary boundaries dashboard
- NASA Earth Observations (EO) indicators
- European Space Agency (ESA) Climate Change Initiative
- Global Carbon Project
- Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC) special reports (when applicable)

---

## A.3 Sovereign Override

The Sovereign Customer retains the right to delay activation of the auto-governance provisions for up to **72 hours** for national security review. However, the Customer cannot permanently veto a time-locked ecological provision once the trigger conditions are met. After the 72-hour window, the throttling provisions shall execute automatically unless the trigger conditions have been resolved.

---

## A.4 Throttling Actions

Upon activation, the Asha Linter shall:

- Reduce non-essential compute workloads by a minimum of 20% (or to the level needed to remain within the safe boundary).
- Divert surplus power to the NERM and AHM modules to accelerate water purification and fertilizer recovery.
- Pause high-energy AI training jobs that are not time-critical.
- Notify all participating sovereigns via the Optical Ledger.

---

## A.5 Audit Trail

All triggers, overrides, and throttling actions shall be recorded immutably on the Optical Ledger, with aggregated data made publicly available.

---

## A.6 Implementation Path

This annex is optional. It shall be activated only by mutual written agreement of the parties, and its technical specifications shall be finalized by the Joint Working Group prior to the first node deployment.
