## 1. Overview

The Nutrient & Energy Recovery Module (NERM) is the core metabolic component of a GRII node. It converts municipal or industrial wastewater into:

- **Clean greywater** for cooling, irrigation, or industrial use
- **Struvite (MgNH₄PO₄)** – a slow‑release phosphorus fertilizer
- **Ammonium sulfate** – a nitrogen fertilizer
- **Biogas** – renewable energy
- **Micro‑hydro power** – from internal flow

All structural components are 3D‑printed from BAETA polymer, enabling the module to also capture CO₂ and harvest water vapor from air (see BAETA Advanced Air Module).

---

## 2. Core Technologies

### 2.1 Ceramic Membrane Bioreactor (CMBR)

- **Function:** High‑efficiency solid‑liquid separation, producing a concentrated nutrient slurry and clean permeate.
- **Advantage:** Eliminates the need for energy‑intensive pre‑concentration; increases phosphorus recovery by 15‑20%.
- **Scale:** 13 MGD (pilot) → 72 MGD (full scale).

### 2.2 Fluidized Bed Reactor (FBR)

- **Input:** Nutrient‑rich slurry from CMBR.
- **Output:** High‑purity **struvite** crystals (sold as slow‑release P fertilizer).
- **Recovery:** 80‑90% of incoming phosphorus.

### 2.3 Ammonium Sulfate Recovery

- **Process:** Stripping and scrubbing of ammonia from digestate.
- **Output:** **Ammonium sulfate** solution or crystals (N fertilizer).
- **Recovery:** 60‑80% of incoming nitrogen.

### 2.4 Anaerobic Digester

- **Input:** Organic fraction of sludge, food waste.
- **Output:** Biogas (methane/CO₂) for electricity or heat.
- **Integration:** Digester heat can be used to maintain optimal temperatures for other processes.

### 2.5 Micro‑Hydro Turbines

- **Placement:** Installed in the primary discharge pipe or within the vertical farm circulation loop.
- **Capacity:** 50–150 kW continuous (at 13 MGD) → 250–750 kW at full 72 MGD.
- **Benefit:** Offsets electricity demand of pumps, lighting, and nutrient recovery equipment.
