## B.1 Extraterrestrial Data Classification

Data generated by Enclave-derived systems operating beyond Earth's atmosphere (e.g., Mare Tranquillitatis ISRU payload, orbital relay stations, Martian forward bases) shall be classified as **"Sovereign Extraterrestrial Data"** and governed by the originating Customer's Cultural Invariants, unless otherwise agreed in a bilateral space accord.

### Classification Hierarchy

| Data Type | Classification | Governance |
|-----------|---------------|------------|
| **Operational Telemetry** | Sovereign Extraterrestrial Data | Customer's Cultural Invariants apply |
| **Scientific Discovery** | Sovereign (12-month window) then Celestial Commons | See Section B.3 |
| **Navigation/Safety** | Celestial Commons (immediate) | Shared openly per COPUOS obligations |
| **Commercial/Industrial** | Sovereign Extraterrestrial Data | Customer's Cultural Invariants apply |
| **Medical/Life Support** | Sovereign Extraterrestrial Data with emergency sharing | Emergency override for crew safety |

## B.2 Earth-Moon Data Handshake

Data transmitted between Earth-based Enclaves and extraterrestrial modules shall be:

- **Encrypted** using the same Sovereign Keys and post-quantum algorithms (CRYSTALS-Kyber/Dilithium) defined in Section 2.2.1 of the core Addendum.
- **Latency-Aware:** The Asha Linter shall account for Earth-Moon communication delay (~1.3 seconds one-way) in attestation protocols. Attestation windows shall be extended proportionally for extraterrestrial modules.
- **Redundantly Routed:** All Earth-Moon data shall traverse at least two independent communication paths (e.g., NASA Deep Space Network + Chinese Deep Space Network or commercial relay constellation).

### Key Management for Extraterrestrial Operations

| Scenario | Key Authority | Failover |
|----------|--------------|----------|

## B.3 Celestial Commons Clause

Scientific data of broad human benefit generated by Enclave-derived extraterrestrial systems shall be shared openly under the following terms:

### Qualifying Data Categories
- Lunar geology, regolith composition, and subsurface mapping
- Cosmic radiation measurements and shielding effectiveness
- In-situ resource utilization yields and process data
- Atmospheric and environmental data (Mars, other bodies)
- Biological experiment results in reduced gravity

### Sharing Protocol

1. **Proprietary Window:** The originating Sovereign Adapter retains exclusive access for **12 months** from data generation.
2. **Publication:** After the proprietary window, data shall be published to the Optical Ledger with full attribution to the originating Sovereign Adapter and its Cultural Invariants.
3. **Attribution:** All derivative works, publications, and applications based on Celestial Commons data must credit the originating Sovereign Adapter.
4. **No Weaponization:** Celestial Commons data shall not be used for weapons development, military targeting, or offensive capability enhancement by any party.

### Exception: Navigation and Safety Data
Navigation, orbital mechanics, and crew safety data shall be shared **immediately** with no proprietary window, consistent with COPUOS (Committee on the Peaceful Uses of Outer Space) obligations and the Outer Space Treaty (1967).

## B.4 Relationship to Existing Space Law

This Annex operates within and supplements the following international frameworks:

| Framework | Relationship |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Outer Space Treaty (1967)** | Consistent with Article I (space for benefit of all), Article II (no national appropriation), Article IX (cooperation) |
| **Moon Agreement (1979)** | Compatible; data governance does not constitute territorial claim |
| **Artemis Accords (2020)** | Compatible with Section 10 (interoperability) and Section 11 (deconfliction of activities) |
| **COPUOS Guidelines** | Fully consistent with long-term sustainability guidelines |

This Annex does not constitute a territorial claim, resource claim, or assertion of sovereignty over any celestial body. It governs only the **data generated by Enclave-derived systems**, not the physical environment in which they operate.

## B.5 Dispute Resolution

Disputes arising under this Annex shall be resolved by the Court of Last Resort, with the following modifications for extraterrestrial operations:

- **Expedited Timeline:** Given the operational constraints of space missions, disputes shall be resolved within **30 days** (vs. standard timeline).
- **Technical Panel:** The arbitration panel shall include at least one member with demonstrated expertise in space operations, orbital mechanics, or planetary science.
- **Interim Measures:** Pending resolution, the disputed data shall remain encrypted under the originating Customer's Sovereign Keys, with no access by any party.
