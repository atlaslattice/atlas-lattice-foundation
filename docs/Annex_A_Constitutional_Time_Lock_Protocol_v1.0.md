## A.1 Trigger Conditions

Certain provisions of the Sovereign Enclave Legal Addendum (e.g., Planetary Boundary Oracles per Section 8.4, Auto-Throttling of resource-intensive compute) shall activate automatically when **3 or more** independent, verifiable Earth-monitoring networks concurrently report that a planetary boundary in the Enclave's operating region has entered the "high-risk" zone.

### Qualifying Monitoring Networks

The following networks are pre-approved as independent data sources for trigger evaluation:

| Network | Domain | Authority |
|---------|--------|-----------|
| Stockholm Resilience Centre | Planetary boundaries framework | Academic / Intergovernmental |
| NASA Earth Observations (NEO) | Satellite-based Earth systems monitoring | US Government / Scientific |
| ESA Climate Office | European Earth observation (Copernicus) | EU Intergovernmental |
| ISRO Earth Observation Programme | South Asian regional monitoring | Indian Government / Scientific |
| China National Space Administration (CNSA) | Asia-Pacific regional monitoring | Chinese Government / Scientific |

Additional networks may be added by mutual written agreement between the Customer and Provider, subject to independence verification by the Court of Last Resort.

### Boundary Categories

The following planetary boundary categories are recognized for trigger evaluation:

1. **Freshwater Use** - Regional depletion approaching unsafe limits
2. **Nitrogen/Phosphorus Cycle** - Biogeochemical flow disruption
3. **Thermal Pollution** - Local heat island or water thermal discharge exceeding safe thresholds
4. **Biodiversity Loss** - Regional species loss rate exceeding background extinction rate by >10x
5. **Land-System Change** - Deforestation or land degradation within Enclave watershed
6. **Atmospheric Aerosol Loading** - Regional air quality index exceeding WHO guidelines for >30 consecutive days

## A.2 Sovereign Override

The Customer may delay activation of a time-locked provision for up to **72 hours** for review and verification of the trigger data. During this review period:

- The Customer may request raw data from the triggering monitoring networks.
- The Asha Linter shall generate a technical assessment of the trigger event.
- The Provider shall provide any relevant operational data from the Enclave.

The Customer **cannot permanently veto** a time-locked provision once trigger conditions are confirmed by 3+ independent networks. If the Customer disputes the data, the matter shall be referred to the Court of Last Resort for expedited arbitration (target: 7-day resolution).

---

## A.3 Graduated Response

Upon activation, the following graduated response shall apply:

| Alert Level | Condition | Response |
|-------------|-----------|----------|
| **YELLOW** | 1-2 networks report high-risk | Advisory issued to Customer and Provider; no automatic action |
| **ORANGE** | 3+ networks report high-risk | 72-hour Customer review window begins; Asha Linter prepares throttle plan |
| **RED** | 3+ networks confirmed + review window expired or Customer acknowledges | Automatic compute throttling per Section 8.4; non-essential workloads paused |
| **CRITICAL** | Physical danger to Enclave or irreversible environmental harm imminent | Kill switch standby; full coordination with Customer per Section 5 |

---

## A.4 Restoration

Once the triggering boundary returns to "safe" zone as confirmed by 2+ of the original triggering networks, the time-locked provisions shall deactivate automatically. The Asha Linter shall restore full compute capacity over a **24-hour ramp-up period** to prevent system shock.

## A.5 Audit Trail

All trigger events, overrides, activations, graduated responses, and restorations shall be recorded on the Optical Ledger with:

- Cryptographic proof of the underlying monitoring data
- Timestamp of each state transition
- Identity of reviewing parties
- Asha Linter assessment reports
- Customer override requests and outcomes
