# National Defense Dividend Act (NDDA) — Legislative Scaffold

**Status:** Draft Legislative Framework | **Date:** January 10, 2026  
**Classification:** UNCLASSIFIED // FOR PUBLIC RELEASE

---

## Executive Summary

The NDDA reframes healthcare reform as a **national security imperative**, treating the private insurance industry's $3–4 trillion annual extraction as a **domestic sovereignty threat** equivalent to hostile economic occupation.

**Core Thesis:** An extractive, non-productive industry that disables national productivity, suppresses innovation, and blocks sovereign resilience is a national security threat requiring defense-level response.

---

## Title I: Healthcare Clawback & Fraud Recovery

### Sec. 101 — Healthcare Clawback Fund

**(a) 40-Year Fraud Audit** (1985–2025):
- AI-powered pattern detection for systematic fraud
- Statistical sampling methodology for claims review
- Clawback authority for fraudulent denials with 3x damages

**(b) National Whistleblower Bounty Program:**
- 15–30% bounty on recovered funds
- Protected channels (zero-knowledge architecture)
- AI review system for rapid claim validation
- Modeled on IRS and False Claims Act protocols

### Sec. 102 — Recovery Targets

- Conservative: **$3.2–8T over 5 years** ($640B–$1.6T/year)
- Allocation: 40% Care Guarantee / 30% Sovereign Dividend / 20% Defense Infrastructure / 10% Whistleblower bounties

---

## Title II: Citizen Care Guarantee

- Every citizen: 24/7 AI diagnostic triage, annual preventive care, zero point-of-service fees for primary care
- 10,000 Sovereign Health Nodes nationwide
- Open-source medical AI on public datasets
- **Cost: $575B/year vs current $4.5T = $3.9T/year savings**

---

## Title III: Sovereign Dividend Fund

**Annual Dividend Schedule:**
- Year 1–2: $500–1,000/citizen/year
- Year 3–5: $1,500–2,500/citizen/year
- Year 6+: $2,500–3,400/citizen/year

**Sovereign Hardware Kit** (every citizen):
- Chromebook (secure compute, $300 value)
- Smartphone (secure communication, $600 value)
- Biometric node (health monitoring, $200 value)
